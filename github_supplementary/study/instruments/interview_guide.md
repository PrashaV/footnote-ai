# Semi-Structured Interview Guide
**Footnote User Study**  
Duration: ~15 minutes | Conducted: Remotely via video conference  
Audio recorded with participant consent | Transcribed verbatim

---

## Interviewer Notes

This guide provides structure while allowing natural conversation. Do **not** read questions verbatim. Use probing phrases liberally:
- *"Can you tell me more about that?"*
- *"Can you give me a specific example?"*
- *"What did you mean by [X]?"*
- *"How did that compare to the other tool?"*

Allow silence — participants often elaborate if you wait 2–3 seconds before prompting.

---

## D.1 Opening (2 min)

> "Thank you for completing both tasks today. I'd like to ask you a few questions about your experience. There are no right or wrong answers — I'm genuinely interested in your honest reactions, including criticisms. This should take about 15 minutes. Is it okay if I continue recording for transcription purposes?"

*(Confirm consent to record before proceeding.)*

---

## D.2 Overall Experience (3 min)

**OE1.** How would you describe your overall experience completing the tasks today — with both tools?

**OE2.** Which tool did you find more useful for this particular kind of task, and why?

**OE3.** Were there any moments during either task where you felt frustrated or confused?  
> *Probe: What caused that? How did you get past it?*

**OE4.** Did anything surprise you — positively or negatively — about either tool?

---

## D.3 Feature-Specific Questions: Footnote (5 min)

**F1.** Which feature of Footnote did you find most valuable, and why?

**F2.** How did you use the citation knowledge graph?  
> *Probe: Did it help you navigate the literature? Did you click on nodes? Did you use the pan/zoom? Was it overwhelming or immediately useful?*

**F3.** Did you notice the confidence ratings on the key findings (the low/medium/high badges)?  
> *Probe: How did you use them? Did they influence which papers you looked at? Did you trust them?*

**F4.** Did you look at the research gaps panel?  
> *Probe: Were the gaps useful? Were they too obvious, or did they suggest genuinely new directions?*

**F5.** Did you use the narrative literature review output or the methodology comparison table?  
> *Probe: Would you use either of those in your actual research workflow? Why/why not?*

**F6.** Is there a feature you wish Footnote had that it currently lacks?  
> *Probe: Something that would make it significantly more useful in your actual workflow?*

---

## D.4 Trust and Concerns (3 min)

**T1.** How much did you trust the information generated by Footnote?  
> *Probe: Did you verify anything independently during the task? What triggered that verification?*

**T2.** Do you have any concerns about using AI-generated summaries in your actual research work?  
> *Probe: Are there specific situations where you would or wouldn't use it?*

**T3.** Were there specific moments where you weren't sure whether to trust Footnote's output?  
> *Probe: What caused that uncertainty? How did you resolve it?*

**T4.** Footnote grounds its outputs in real retrieved papers — does knowing that change how much you trust it?  
> *Probe: Does seeing the source papers listed alongside findings help?*

---

## D.5 Adoption and Future Use (2 min)

**A1.** Can you see yourself using Footnote in your real research workflow?  
> *Probe: For what kinds of tasks? How often? Would it replace or supplement what you currently do?*

**A2.** Would you recommend Footnote to a colleague? What would you say to them?

**A3.** What would need to change about Footnote for you to use it regularly?

---

## D.6 Closing

> "Is there anything else you'd like to share about your experience today — anything I haven't asked about that felt important?"

> "Thank you very much for your time and for your thoughtful, honest responses. Your feedback will directly shape the development of this system and the paper we're writing about it."

*(End recording. Note any off-the-record comments if participant offers them.)*
